Problem

Between appointments,
care goes quiet.

Oncology patients live chemotherapy cycles in the gaps between clinic visits. During those gaps, symptoms worsen in silence, patients hesitate to call, and clinical teams learn too late. The first 200 ms of any interface must say safe before it says useful. Today, most don’t.

70%
of treatment-related symptoms are under-reported to the care team, according to PRO studies
14–21d
gap between oncology check-ups during active chemotherapy
≤14d
before a worsening symptom is recognised, when it is recognised at all

Self-reports, paper diaries and call-centres haven’t moved the needle. The space between appointments is where the most clinically meaningful things happen, and where patients are most alone.

Solution

Silver lives in
that gap.

Three senses, a calendar, and a clinician. Silver accompanies the patient every day, notices what the care team can’t, and hands over the moment a specialist is needed.

01

Voice

Daily check-ins by voice. Ninety seconds, no forms, no scales. The companion speaks Portuguese, listens, and extracts the clinical signal silently.

02

Body

Continuous wearable signals from Apple Health, Google Health Connect and Whoop. Sleep, HRV, resting HR, strain, all unified into a single recovery score.

03

Words

Text Q&A for the quiet questions about treatment, symptoms and side effects. Grounded in the patient’s actual data, not a generic chatbot.

+1

A clinician

A specialist always on the other side of the screen. The AI handles the everyday; the clinician steps in on red flags, on uncertainty, and whenever the patient asks for it.

Foundation

Seven principles
older than software.

Silver inherits from architects and designers who understood that space, light and silence are also medicine.

01

Pallasmaa

"The eyes want to collaborate with the other senses."

Design beyond the visual. Breathing animations at 4-second intervals, a normal resting respiratory rate. Transitions with weight, never instant. Designed for haptic feedback on state changes.

02

Albers

Color as relationship, never as decoration.

No clinical blue. A warm sand base lets emotional accents speak. Each pastel is a climate, not a label. Rose, lavender, sage, cream, tuned like chords.

03

Alexander

Spaces that adapt to who arrives.

Three density modes. Light on bad days: larger type, narrower column, fewer affordances. Medium on normal days. Open on good days, when the patient wants more.

04

Norman

The visceral layer survives pain.

No menus to parse. No decisions to make. The orb is the hero. The patient opens the app and finds a presence, not a tool. Tap. Speak. Breathe back.

05

Sternberg

The effort of existing in a space is a clinical variable.

Never "De 1 a 10". CTCAE is inferred, never asked. One question per message. Always. The patient's own words become the system's words.

06

De Botton

Spaces reflecting who we want to be, not what we must do.

The patient is not a case. The screen is not a form. Silver returns identity, not compliance metrics. PT-PT, "tu", never "você".

07

Zumthor

The void is generous. Silence is a feature.

3-to-4 second transitions. Imperceptible. The interface never hurries the patient. The interface never hurries itself. Nothing happens until the patient is ready.

Infusion days

“Amanhã é dia de tratamento. Queres preparar alguma coisa?” Silver knows the treatment protocol, the drugs, and the usual timeline of side effects that follow.

The crash window

On days 2–3 after infusion, Silver is quieter. Fewer questions. Simpler language. Larger type. The interface bends toward the body.

Consults & scans

“Tens consulta na quinta-feira. Queres preparar notas para a Dra?” Silver helps the patient arrive informed, with the week’s signals already organised.

Blood work & exams

Holds space for pre-scan anxiety without amplifying it. Gentle reminders, never nags. The calendar is a companion, not a taskmaster.

Cycle memory

Designed to learn how each patient’s body responds over cycles. If the fatigue peaks on day three, Silver notes it, and so does the clinician.

Silence as a signal

If the patient goes quiet during a crash window, that’s expected. If they go quiet during a stable week, that’s a signal worth flagging.

Clinical substrate

Guardian ↔ Responder

On bad days, the companion is a guardian: quiet, protective, low-burden. On good days, a responder: curious, expansive, willing to go deeper. The spectrum is continuous, modulated by mood, symptom load and wearable recovery.

Guardian mode · low bandwidth

Single-gesture check-in. "É suficiente?" / "Quero falar." Larger type. Narrower affordances. Recovery score front and centre. CTCAE inferred silently from minimal input.

Responder mode · high bandwidth

Full conversation. Symptom exploration in the patient's own words. Trend review. Treatment calendar awareness. FOLFOX / FOLFIRI / AC / CAPECITABINE / PEMBROLIZUMAB profiles.

CTCAE v5 · inferred, never asked

The patient speaks naturally. The system infers CTCAE grades from the conversation, validated against a gold case corpus and specialist ratings.

Memory across sessions

Last five encounters loaded at session start. The companion remembers: "Ontem disseste-me que o jardim te fez bem." Not surveillance, continuity.

Clinician oversight

The moment a
clinician steps in.

Silver does most of the everyday work alone: the gentle check-ins, the symptom tracking, the questions about treatment. But the AI knows where its edges are. A dedicated severe adverse-event algorithm runs on every encounter, and when something crosses a line, Silver acts. Fast, with full context, without the patient having to ask twice.

Tier 1 · severe AE

Responder → hospital.

When the responder identifies a severe adverse event (febrile neutropenia, suspected sepsis, chest pain, breathlessness, uncontrolled bleeding, neurological red flags, anaphylaxis) the AI does not improvise. It tells the patient, in their own language, to go to the hospital now, and simultaneously alerts the care team that the patient is on the way.

  • Immediate, unambiguous instruction to the patient
  • Automatic notification to the oncology service
  • Companion stays with the patient through the transition
  • Encounter tagged as SAE · hospital-directed in the audit trail
Tier 2 · moderate AE

Clinician notified.

For less severe but still serious adverse events, grade 2–3 symptoms that don’t demand the ER but do demand attention, Silver does not send the patient anywhere. It pushes a notification to the clinician: the symptom, the trend, the wearable context, the cycle day, the AI’s recommended next step. The clinician decides.

  • Real-time notification to the named clinician
  • Context bundle: last 7 days of signals, symptoms, transcripts
  • Patient informed that the team has been told
  • Follow-up check-in prompted after resolution

Patient request

At any moment the patient can say “quero falar com a minha equipa” and the conversation routes to a clinician. No friction, no form, no waiting room. The AI summarises what has happened so the specialist walks in already informed.

Model uncertainty

When the model is not confident (low extraction certainty, ambiguous symptom, contradictory signals) it stops pretending. It flags the encounter for review and defers the interpretation to a person.

Silence

When the patient stops checking in for longer than they usually do, Silver notices. Outreach first, then a nudge to the care team. A missing voice is a clinical signal too.

Drug interactions

Every symptom is cross-checked against the patient’s current medications and comorbidities. A benign cough on one protocol is a red flag on another. The rules live in code, reviewed by clinicians.

Crisis detection

A dedicated safety layer screens every encounter for suicidal ideation and acute psychological crisis, graded from low to critical. When triggered, Silver connects the patient to the national crisis line (808 200 204) and alerts the care team immediately.

Caregiver as proxy

Silver detects when a family member or caregiver is speaking on behalf of the patient. The conversation adapts, the clinical extraction adjusts, and the encounter is flagged so the clinician knows the source.

Full context on handoff

The clinician opens Silver and sees the last seven days of signals, symptoms and transcripts. Not a blank screen.

The AI stays present

Escalation doesn’t end the companion. It keeps the patient company while the clinician approaches.

Every handoff logged

Audit trail of what the AI saw, what it decided, and when the clinician took over. Transparency by default.

The loop

Evaluated in three phases.

Every change to Silver (a new prompt, a new model, a new safety rule) runs the full three-phase evaluation before it reaches a patient. Nothing ships on intuition.

A

Baseline

Canonical gold cases across every symptom class, with ground-truth CTCAE labels. The sanity layer. Nothing ships below baseline.

B

Variants

Prompt variations, model swaps, ambient signal experiments. Severity-distribution analysis. Patient exposure tracking.

C

Field

Real-world clinical encounters. Evaluable vs non-evaluable. Rater agreement. Longitudinal outcomes.

Gold corpus

Hand-curated cases spanning every symptom class, used as the sanity layer.

CTCAE v5

Corpus-grade terms with inferred severity from natural speech.

Specialist review

Structured rating forms per encounter, exportable for analysis.

Observability

Tokens, latency, cost, trace per turn. Every decision auditable.
